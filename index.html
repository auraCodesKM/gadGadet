Why monitoring is non-negotiable

01

Uncontrolled glucose is the primary driver of kidney failure, adult blindness, nerve damage, and cardiovascular disease in Indian diabetics — all largely preventable with consistent monitoring.

IDF · 2023
02

Daily glucose tracking is the single most effective intervention for preventing complications — more than diet, exercise, or any single medication.

Lancet · 2021
03

NEJM · 2019

02 — The pain of current monitoring

The standard method — finger-prick glucometry — requires puncturing your fingertip with a lancet multiple times every day, for the rest of your life.

01

Recommended frequency is 2–4 tests daily — up to 1,460 lancet punctures per year. Each requires a blood-soaked disposable strip that is discarded immediately.

ADA Guidelines
02

Pain aversion and needle anxiety are the primary self-reported barriers to compliance. Not cost. Not knowledge. Pain.

Diabetic Med · 2022
03

Long-term lancet use causes fingertip scarring and peripheral nerve desensitisation — the very condition patients are trying to prevent through monitoring.

J Diabetes Sci · 2020
04

The glucometer strip market costs patients ₹4,500/month in consumables alone — a recurring cost borne disproportionately by middle-income Indian households with no ceiling.

Market analysis

Downstream consequences of under-monitoring

Diabetic retinopathy — leading cause of adult blindness in India (4.2M active cases)
Diabetic nephropathy — fastest growing driver of kidney failure requiring dialysis
Undetected hypoglycaemia — causes accidents, loss of consciousness, and cardiac events
Delayed insulin adjustment — silent damage accumulates over months before clinical symptoms appear
